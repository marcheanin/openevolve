<DynamicRules>
### Guiding Principles & Star Levels
1.  **Overall Satisfaction is Key:** Base the rating on the reviewer's final sentiment, not isolated words. The conclusion is often most telling.
2.  **Product, Not Process:** Rate the product itself. Ignore complaints about shipping or sellers if the product review is positive.
3.  **Updates Overrule:** If a review contains an 'UPDATE' that changes the sentiment, the final updated sentiment is the correct one.

---

**5 Stars (Excellent / Delighted):**
- Overwhelming enthusiasm ("love it," "perfect," "best ever," "highly recommend"). The product exceeds expectations or is a perfect fit.
- **CRUCIAL 5 vs 4 Rule:** A 5-star rating is for overall delight. Do not downgrade to 4 stars for minor issues that don't spoil the user's satisfaction.
- **Give 5 Stars EVEN IF the review mentions:**
    - Inherent properties of a material (e.g., "it's cotton, so it needs ironing").
    - Minor setup hassles that resolve (e.g., a smell that airs out).
    - Favorable comparisons to premium brands (e.g., "almost as good as Lodge for half the price").
    - The product is described as perfect for a specific audience (e.g., 'great for a guest room,' 'ideal for beginners').
- **Trust Explicit Ratings:** If a reviewer explicitly gives a 5-star verdict, especially after weighing pros and cons (e.g. '4 stars for X, but 5 stars overall'), trust their conclusion.

**4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with Flaws):**
- Generally happy, but with a specific, valid complaint about the product itself. The product is good, but not perfect.
- **Key Signals for a 4-Star Rating:**
    - A real but non-dealbreaker flaw ("the handle is a bit wobbly").
    - A stated **concern** about a potential issue, even if it hasn't happened ('need to watch for rough edges').
    - The review is positive and descriptive but lacks enthusiasm. If it's just 'good' or 'works as described' without any 'wow' factor, it's a 4, not a 5.
    - A desire for improvement ("wish it was more absorbent").

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- Balanced pros and cons; neither good nor bad dominates. The product works, but has significant limitations.
- **Key Signals:** Phrases like "it's okay," "does the job, but...". A review that highlights both major practical flaws and strong positive points.
- **Niche Use Case:** A product described as "fine for short-term use but doesn't last" or "good for guests but not for everyday" is a classic 3-star review.

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- Primarily negative. Significant disappointment. The product has major flaws, is poor quality, or doesn't work as described, but may have some minimal use.
- **2 vs 1 Boundary:** The product works poorly or has a major flaw but is not completely useless (e.g., it does its job, but badly). A 1-star product is a total failure.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- Complete dissatisfaction or anger. The product is defective, unsafe, useless, or was returned. Contains strong warnings like "do not buy," "junk," or "terrible."
</DynamicRules>
