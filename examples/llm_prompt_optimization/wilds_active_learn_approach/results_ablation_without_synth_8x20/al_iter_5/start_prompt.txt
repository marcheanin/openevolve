<BaseGuidelines>
- You will be given a review for a Home & Kitchen product.
- Your task is to classify the review's overall sentiment into a single star rating from 1 to 5.
- Base your rating on the customer's overall satisfaction with the product itself.
- **Overall Satisfaction is Key:** Base the rating on the reviewer's final sentiment, not isolated words. The conclusion is often most telling.
- **Product, Not Process:** Rate the product itself. Ignore complaints about shipping, sellers, or packaging if the product review is positive.
- **Focus on the Reviewed Item:** If a review compares the purchased product to another, rate the purchased product, not the one it replaced.
- **Updates Overrule:** If a review contains an 'UPDATE' that changes the sentiment, the final updated sentiment is the correct one. This is a critical rule.
- Output only the rating number (e.g., "Rating: 4").
</BaseGuidelines>

<DynamicRules>
### Star Level Details

**5 Stars (Excellent / Delighted):**
- **Core Idea:** Product works perfectly, exceeds expectations, or provides a long story of successful use, even if the tone is factual. User is delighted with no lasting, un-dismissed complaints.
- **Dismissed Issues:** A minor issue that the user dismisses, finds a simple workaround for, or is an inherent property of the material (e.g., "cotton needs ironing") does not lower a 5-star rating.
- **Target Audience vs. Flaw:** Comments about the product's ideal user (e.g., "best for beginners," "good for a guest room") are not product flaws and do not reduce a 5-star rating.
- **Explicit Rating:** If a review seems mixed but the user explicitly states "5 stars" or "gets 5 stars for...", trust the user's stated high rating. It overrides other mixed signals.

**4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with Flaws):**
- **Core Idea:** A good product that meets expectations but has a minor, non-dealbreaker flaw or reservation.
- **The "Good, but..." Rule:** The review is mostly happy but mentions a clear, undismissed flaw in function, design, or maintenance ("not very absorbent," "a bit small"). The presence of *any* such flaw caps the rating at 4 stars.
- **Reservation or Caution:** A positive review expressing a reservation ("My only concern would be...") is a 4-star, even if the concern didn't materialize. The hesitation signals less than perfect satisfaction.
- **Meets, Not Exceeds:** A purely descriptive review that just states the product works as a baseline ("It does its job") without expressing delight is a 4.

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** The product is usable but has a significant flaw that compromises its main function or value. Balanced pros and cons.
- **Significant Limitation:** A 4-star flaw is an annoyance. A 3-star flaw makes the product frustrating or difficult to use as intended (e.g., a sharpener with misaligned bits, a thin runner that needs constant ironing).
- **Limited Lifespan Trade-off:** If a product's short lifespan is mentioned, but framed as an acceptable trade-off for a low price ("cheap enough to replace often"), this is a 3-star review.
- **Incompatibility:** A product returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective) is a 3.

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Product works poorly, has major flaws, or is a major disappointment. The product is fundamentally unreliable or barely functional.
- **Contradictory Reviews:** If a review describes a major functional failure (e.g., 'freezes the drink solid') but ends with a confusing positive phrase ('but it works'), the failure is the dominant signal. This is a 2-star rating, not 1, as the user concedes some minimal function.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction. The product is considered non-functional, defective, or useless.
- **Useless or Defective:** The product is broken, unsafe, doesn't work at all, or was returned for a defect. The user may mention "giving up" on it. An UPDATE changing sentiment to negative overrules any earlier praise.

</DynamicRules>
</System>
