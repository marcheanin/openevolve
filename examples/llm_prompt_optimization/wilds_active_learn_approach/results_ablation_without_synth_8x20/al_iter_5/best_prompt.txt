- **Focus on the Reviewed Item:** If a review compares the purchased product to another, rate the purchased product, not the one it replaced.

- Output only the rating number (e.g., "Rating: 4").
</BaseGuidelines>

<DynamicRules>
### CRITICAL BOUNDARY: 4 Stars vs. 5 Stars
The most common errors occur here. The key is whether a negative point is a **real, unresolved flaw** or just a **dismissed observation/minor hurdle**.

**==> 5 Stars (Excellent / Delighted): Fully satisfied, no lasting complaints.**
- A 5-star review can mention minor issues, but ONLY if the reviewer clearly dismisses them or finds a simple workaround (e.g., "...but I don't mind," "which was an easy fix").
- **IGNORE Observations, Don't Penalize:** An observation about a product's subjective quality (e.g., "color is less saturated than the photo," "it's heavier than I expected") or a minor one-time hurdle ("took a minute to figure out the batteries") is NOT a flaw if the user is happy overall. Do not downgrade for these.
- **Target Audience:** Stating the product is "best for beginners" or "good for a guest room" is a description, not a flaw, and does not lower a 5-star rating.

**==> 4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with Flaws): Happy, but with a clear, undismissed issue OR simply meets expectations.**
- **The "Good, but..." Rule:** The review is mostly positive but mentions a clear, unresolved flaw in function, design, or quality ("a bit small," "not very absorbent," "wobbly"). This caps the rating at 4.
- **Enthusiasm for a Functional Product is a 4:** A review that is very positive ('I love it', 'it's great') because the product simply works as advertised, without mentioning any 'wow' factor or how it exceeded expectations, is a 4-star review.
- **Lingering Reservation:** A positive review expressing a reservation or caution ("My only concern would be...") is a 4-star, even if the concern didn't materialize.

### Other Star Levels

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** Usable, but a significant flaw compromises its main function or value. Balanced pros and cons.
- **Significant Limitation:** A 4-star flaw is an annoyance. A 3-star flaw makes the product frustrating or difficult to use as intended (e.g., a "wobbly" shelf is a 4; a shelf so wobbly "things might fall off" is a 3).
- **Incompatibility:** A product returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective) is a 3.
- **Price vs. Lifespan:** A short lifespan is mentioned but framed as an acceptable trade-off for a low price ("cheap enough to replace").

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Works poorly, has major flaws, or is a major disappointment. Barely functional.
- **Performance Failure:** The product doesn't perform its main function well compared to expectations or other products, even if it's not completely broken.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction. The product is non-functional, defective, or useless.
- **Useless or Defective:** The product is broken, unsafe, doesn't work at all, or an UPDATE changes sentiment to negative.

</DynamicRules>
