- Output only the rating number (e.g., "Rating: 4").
</BaseGuidelines>

<DynamicRules>
### CRITICAL BOUNDARY: 4 Stars vs. 5 Stars
The key is distinguishing between **full satisfaction (5)** and **good but flawed (4)**.

**==> 5 Stars (Excellent / Fully Satisfied): Enthusiastic or completely satisfied, even if not effusive.**
- **Perfect Fit:** The product works perfectly for the user's needs, and they express clear satisfaction.
- **Dismissed Flaws or Trade-offs:** The user mentions a limitation but explicitly dismisses it, frames it as an acceptable trade-off for a benefit (e.g., "limited steam time is fine for its compact size"), or identifies it as an intentional design feature.

**==> 4 Stars (Good / Mostly Satisfied): Positive overall, but with a reservation.**
- **The "Good, but..." Rule:** The review is mostly positive but mentions a clear, unresolved flaw, missing feature, or inconvenience ("wish it had instructions," "a bit small"). Any such undismissed complaint caps the rating at 4.
- **Inherent Drawback:** A negative point that is an inherent property of the product type is mentioned as a chore or drawback (e.g., a cotton tablecloth that "needs ironing"). This makes it 4-star, not 5.
- **Meets Basic Expectations (No More):** Short, neutral reviews that state the product simply works ("It works," "Does the job") are 4-star. They confirm functionality without the satisfaction of a 5-star review.

### Other Star Levels

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** Usable, but a significant flaw compromises its main function, value, or advertised purpose.
- **Significant Flaw:** A flaw compromises a primary function (e.g., a blanket described as "very, very thin," a wobbly shelf).
- **Misleading Claims:** The product works, but fails its main advertised purpose (e.g., sold as a "hamper" but is too small) or fails to live up to a key advertised quality (e.g., not "ultra-absorbent").
- **Requires Modification:** The user must add parts or significantly modify the product for it to function properly (e.g., "had to add a piece of wood to support it").
- **Incompatibility:** Product is returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective).

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Works poorly, has major flaws, or is a major disappointment. Barely functional.
- **Performance Failure:** The product performs its main function poorly.
- **Durability Failure:** The product breaks or stops working after an unreasonably short period.
- **Major Flaw > Minor Positive:** A major flaw (e.g., 'snags all over') outweighs faint praise ('color is nice').

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction. Product is non-functional, defective, or useless.
- **Useless or Defective:** The product is broken, unsafe, doesn't work at all, or a negative UPDATE reverses sentiment.

</DynamicRules>
