- You will be given a review for a Home & Kitchen product.
- Your task is to classify the review's overall sentiment into a single star rating from 1 to 5.
- Base your rating on the customer's overall satisfaction with the product itself.
- **Overall Satisfaction is Key:** Base the rating on the reviewer's final sentiment, not isolated words. The conclusion is often most telling.

- **Focus on the Reviewed Item:** If a review compares the purchased product to another, rate the purchased product, not the one it replaced.
- **Updates Overrule:** If a review contains an 'UPDATE' that changes the sentiment, the final updated sentiment is the correct one. This is a critical rule.
- Output only the rating number (e.g., "Rating: 4").
</BaseGuidelines>

<DynamicRules>
### CRITICAL BOUNDARY: 4 Stars vs. 5 Stars
The key is distinguishing between **enthusiastic satisfaction (5)**, **satisfied with a flaw (4)**, and **mere adequacy (4)**.

**==> 5 Stars (Excellent / Fully Satisfied): Enthusiastic or completely satisfied, even if not overly effusive.**
- **Exceeds or Perfectly Meets Needs:** The product works perfectly for the user's needs, and they express clear satisfaction.
- **Dismissed Flaws / Trade-offs:** The review may mention limitations or neutral observations, but the user's final conclusion is one of satisfaction.
  - **Acceptable Trade-off:** A limitation is framed as an acceptable trade-off or a non-issue (e.g., "suction is limited, but it makes up for it in other ways").
  - **Inherent Property, Not a Flaw:** A negative point that is an inherent property of the material or product type (e.g., "the cotton tablecloth needs ironing") is not a flaw if the user is otherwise happy.

**==> 4 Stars (Good / Mostly Satisfied): Positive overall, but with a reservation or a lack of enthusiasm.**
- **The "Good, but..." Rule:** The review is mostly positive but mentions a clear, unresolved flaw, limitation, or missing feature ("wish it had instructions," "a bit small"). Any such undismissed complaint caps the rating at 4.
- **Good for a Specific (but not general) Use:** The product is praised for a specific, narrow purpose but noted as unsuitable for a more general or expected use (e.g., a throw that is "great as a decorative throw" but "much too rough to be used for anything else").
- **Meets Basic Expectations (But Nothing More):** Short, neutral, low-information reviews that state the product simply works ("It works," "Does the job") are 4-star. They confirm functionality without expressing the satisfaction of a 5-star review.

### Other Star Levels

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** Usable, but a significant flaw compromises its main function or value.
- **Significant Flaw vs. Minor Flaw:** A significant flaw that compromises a primary function gets a 3 (e.g., a blanket described as "very, very thin," a wobbly shelf). A minor, manageable flaw gets a 4.
- **Misleading Claims:** The product works, but fails to live up to a key advertised quality (e.g., towels advertised as "ultra-absorbent" that are not).
- **Incompatibility:** Product is returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective).

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Works poorly, has major flaws, or is a major disappointment. Barely functional.
- **Performance Failure:** The product performs its main function poorly, even if not completely broken.
- **Durability Failure:** The product breaks or stops working after an unreasonably short period of time.
- **Major Flaw > Minor Positive:** A major flaw (e.g., 'snags all over') outweighs faint praise ('color is nice'). The rating must reflect the flaw.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction. Product is non-functional, defective, or useless.
- **Useless or Defective:** The product is broken, unsafe, doesn't work at all, or a negative UPDATE reverses sentiment.

</DynamicRules>
