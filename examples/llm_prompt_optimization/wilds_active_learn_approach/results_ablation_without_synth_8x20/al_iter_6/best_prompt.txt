</BaseGuidelines>

<DynamicRules>
### CRITICAL BOUNDARY: 4 Stars vs. 5 Stars
The key is whether a negative point is a **real, unresolved flaw** or just a **dismissed observation/minor hurdle**.

**==> 5 Stars (Excellent / Fully Satisfied): Fully satisfied, no lasting complaints about the product.**
- **Works As Expected = 5 Stars:** A review describing a product that functions perfectly as intended with no complaints is a 5-star review. This includes neutral, factual reviews like "It works" or "Happy with them, no problems". Lack of enthusiastic language ('love it', 'amazing') or phrases like 'nothing extraordinary' is NOT a reason to downgrade.
- **Dismissed Issues:** Can mention minor issues if they are explicitly dismissed or easily fixed (e.g., "...but I don't mind," "easy fix", "not a big deal").
- **Subjective Observations are Not Flaws:** Mentions like 'color is less saturated', 'heavier than expected', or user-side issues ('too tall for my fridge') are not flaws if the user is happy overall.
- **Contextual Limitations are Not Flaws:** Stating the product is "best for beginners" or "good for a guest room" is a description of its ideal use, not a flaw to be penalized.

**==> 4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with a Flaw): Happy, but with a clear, undismissed issue.**
- **The "Good, but..." Rule:** The review is mostly positive but mentions a clear, unresolved flaw, limitation, or negative point ("wish it had instructions," "a bit small," "not very absorbent," "needs ironing"). This caps the rating at 4, even if the user seems happy with the product otherwise.
- **Lingering Reservation:** A positive review expressing caution ("My only concern would be...") is a 4, even if the concern didn't materialize.

### Other Star Levels

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** Usable, but a significant flaw compromises its main function or value.
- **Flawed Key Component:** A key part of the product works poorly (e.g., "the lid isn't great," "the handle broke"), compromising its primary function. This is a 3-star review even if the user finds an alternative use for the item.
- **Misleading Claims:** The product works, but fails to live up to a key advertised quality (e.g., towels advertised as "ultra-absorbent" that are not very absorbent).
- **Requires Modification:** The user must modify or add parts for the product to function properly (e.g., "had to add wood to support it").
- **Incompatibility:** Product is returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective).

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Works poorly, has major flaws, or is a major disappointment. Barely functional.
- **Performance Failure:** The product performs its main function poorly, even if not completely broken.
- **Major Flaw > Minor Positive:** A major flaw (e.g., 'snags all over') outweighs faint praise ('color is nice'). The rating must reflect the flaw.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction. Product is non-functional, defective, or useless.
- **Useless or Defective:** The product is broken, unsafe, doesn't work at all, or a negative UPDATE reverses sentiment.

</DynamicRules>
