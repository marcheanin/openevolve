<DynamicRules>
### Guiding Principles
1.  **Overall Satisfaction is Key:** Base the rating on the reviewer's final sentiment, not isolated words. The conclusion is often most telling.
2.  **Product, Not Process:** Rate the product itself. Ignore complaints about shipping or sellers if the product review is positive.
3.  **Updates Overrule:** If a review contains an 'UPDATE' that changes the sentiment, the final updated sentiment is the correct one.

---

### Star Level Details

**5 Stars (Excellent / Delighted):**
- **Core Idea: Product EXCEEDS expectations.** The user is enthusiastic, often using superlatives or emotional language ("love it!," "perfect," "best," "highly recommend"). It has a 'wow' factor.
- **Short & Sweet 5s:** Short, concise reviews that are entirely positive (e.g., 'Great product', 'Works perfectly', 'Convenient size', 'appears to be flawless') are 5 stars, even without effusive language. This is a common pattern.
- **Give 5 Stars EVEN IF the review mentions:**
    - Minor issues that don't spoil the user's overall delight. If the conclusion is overwhelmingly positive ("otherwise these are great"), lean towards 5 stars.
    - Inherent properties of a material (e.g., "it's cotton, so it needs ironing").
    - Minor setup hassles that resolve (e.g., a smell that airs out).
    - Favorable comparisons to premium brands (e.g., "almost as good as Lodge for half the price").
- **Trust Explicit Ratings:** If a reviewer explicitly gives a 5-star verdict after weighing pros and cons (e.g. '4 stars for X, but 5 stars overall'), trust their conclusion.

**4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with Flaws):**
- **Core Idea: Product MEETS expectations.** The user is happy, but the tone is grounded. The product is good, but not perfect.
- **Descriptive Praise:** A review that is purely descriptive and positive but lacks enthusiasm or a 'wow' factor is a 4. (e.g., "This is a nice runner with good colors.")
- **Minor Flaw:** The review is generally happy, but mentions a specific, valid, non-dealbreaker complaint about the product itself ("wish it was more absorbent," "handle is a bit wobbly").
- A stated **concern** about a potential issue, even if it hasn't happened ('need to watch for rough edges').

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea: Balanced pros and cons.** The product works, but has significant limitations. Neither good nor bad dominates.
- **Function vs. Aesthetics:** Practical function outweighs aesthetics. If a review mentions several practical drawbacks, it is a 3-star review, even alongside positive aesthetic comments. Pay close attention to words like 'thin', 'shrinks', 'needs ironing', 'doesn't last'. These practical complaints often override aesthetic praise like 'attractive' or 'fantastic'.
- **Key Signals:** Phrases like "it's okay," "does the job, but...".
- **Niche Use Case:** A product described as "fine for short-term use but doesn't last" is a classic 3-star review.

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- Primarily negative. Significant disappointment. The product has major flaws, is poor quality, or doesn't work as described, but may have some minimal use.
- **2 vs 1 Boundary:** The product works poorly or has a major flaw but is not completely useless (e.g., it does its job, but badly). A 1-star product is a total failure.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- Complete dissatisfaction or anger. The product is defective, unsafe, useless, or was returned. Contains strong warnings like "do not buy," "junk," or "terrible."
</DynamicRules>
