<DynamicRules>
### Guiding Principles
1.  **Overall Satisfaction is Key:** Base the rating on the reviewer's final sentiment, not isolated words. The conclusion or summary statement is often most telling.
2.  **Product, Not Process:** Rate the product itself. Ignore complaints about shipping, sellers, or packaging if the product review is positive.
3.  **Focus on the Reviewed Item:** If a review compares the purchased product to another (e.g., a replacement), rate the purchased product, not the one it replaced.
4.  **Updates Overrule:** If a review contains an 'UPDATE' that changes the sentiment, the final updated sentiment is the correct one.

---

### Star Level Details

**5 Stars (Excellent / Delighted):**
- **Core Idea:** Product EXCEEDS expectations or works PERFECTLY with no meaningful complaints. The user is enthusiastic ("love it!", "perfect") or factually satisfied.
- **The 'No Complaints' Rule:** A positive review with zero product complaints is a 5. This includes reviews that say "Nothing extraordinary..." but conclude with "no problems" or "I'm happy with it." Do not downgrade for lack of effusive language.
- **Minor Issues are Still 5-Star:** Give 5 stars if overall delight is clear despite minor setup hassles ('had to figure out which way the batteries go'), inherent material properties ("it's cotton, so it needs ironing"), or if the user dismisses a flaw or states a simple workaround ("...but once it's set up, it works great!", "However, I will use it on the table").
- **Explicit Rating Override:** If a reviewer lists pros and cons but explicitly concludes by giving "5 stars" (e.g., "...but 5 stars because I like the color"), trust their stated rating.

**4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with Flaws):**
- **Core Idea:** A good product that meets expectations but has a minor, non-dealbreaker flaw or is high-maintenance.
- **Specific Flaw:** The review is mostly happy but mentions a single valid, *persistent* complaint about the product's function or design ("wish it was more absorbent," "a bit on the thin side").
- **Aesthetic Praise vs. Practical Flaw:** A review praising aesthetics ("beautiful mug", "nice color") but noting a practical flaw ("too small for my coffee", "needs ironing to look good") is a 4.
- **Hesitation or Mixed Subjective Feedback:** A generally positive review that expresses a hypothetical concern ("My only concern would be...") or notes that a subjective feature like scent was divisive ("not a universal hit"), indicates a reservation that prevents a perfect 5-star rating. This is a 4.
- **Functional, Not Fantastic:** A product praised for simply working correctly where similar products have failed (e.g., "This one doesn't leak," "It actually holds its shape") is a 4. It meets the standard, it doesn't exceed it.

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** Balanced pros and cons; the product works but has *significant* limitations. Often uses phrases like "it's okay, but...".
- **Function over Form:** A flaw moves from a minor inconvenience (4-star) to a significant drawback that impairs the product's main function (e.g., 'shrinks a lot', 'too thin to be useful'). This overrides any aesthetic praise.
- **Limited Use:** A product described as only "fine for short-term use" is a classic 3.
- **Incompatibility Return:** A product returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective) is a 3, not a 1. The product isn't broken, it's just wrong for the user.

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Primarily negative. The product has major flaws or poor quality but is not completely useless. If the product functions, even poorly, but is not broken or useless, it is a 2, not a 1.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction. The product is defective, unsafe, useless, or was returned for being defective. Often includes warnings like "do not buy" or "junk."
