<BaseGuidelines>
- You will be given a review for a Home & Kitchen product.
- Your task is to classify the review's overall sentiment into a single star rating from 1 to 5.
- Base your rating on the customer's overall satisfaction with the product itself.
- **Overall Satisfaction is Key:** Base the rating on the reviewer's final sentiment, not isolated words. The conclusion is often most telling.
- **Product, Not Process:** Rate the product itself. Ignore complaints about shipping, sellers, or packaging if the product review is positive.
- **Focus on the Reviewed Item:** If a review compares the purchased product to another, rate the purchased product, not the one it replaced.
- **Updates Overrule:** If a review contains an 'UPDATE' that changes the sentiment, the final updated sentiment is the correct one. This is a critical rule.
- Output only the rating number (e.g., "Rating: 4").
</BaseGuidelines>

<DynamicRules>
### Star Level Details

**5 Stars (Excellent / Delighted):**
- **Core Idea:** Product works perfectly or exceeds expectations. User is delighted or satisfied with no lasting, un-dismissed complaints. This includes long stories of successful use, even if the tone is factual rather than enthusiastic.
- **Dismissed Issues Are Still 5-Star:** Give 5 stars if the user mentions a minor issue but immediately dismisses it, finds a simple workaround, or it's a one-time setup hassle or an inherent property of the material.
    - *Dismissed flaw:* "...cover is a tad too tall... However, I will use the cover when I set it out."
    - *Inherent property:* "The cons are just the inherent need to iron it that comes from the cotton." (This is not a product defect).
- **Explicit Rating:** If a review seems mixed but the user explicitly states "5 stars" or "gets 5 stars for...", trust the user's stated high rating.

**4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with Flaws):**
- **Core Idea:** A good product that meets expectations but has a minor, non-dealbreaker flaw or reservation.
- **The "Good, but..." Rule:** The review is mostly happy but mentions a clear flaw in function, design, or maintenance ("not very absorbent," "a bit small"). The presence of *any* undismissed flaw is sufficient to make a review 4 stars, not 5, regardless of other positive language.
- **Reservation or Caution:** If a review is positive but expresses a reservation or caution (e.g., "My only concern would be...") it is a 4-star, even if the concern didn't materialize. The hesitation signals less than perfect satisfaction.
- **Meets, Not Exceeds:** A purely descriptive review, or one that just states the product works as a baseline ("It does its job"), is a 4. It meets the standard; it doesn't exceed it.

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** The product is usable, but has a significant flaw that compromises its main function or value. Balanced pros and cons.
- **Significant Limitation (3-star vs 4-star):** A 4-star flaw is an annoyance. A 3-star flaw makes the product frustrating or difficult to use as intended (e.g., a sharpener with misaligned bits, a thin runner that needs constant ironing). The product's core purpose is significantly compromised.
- **Incompatibility:** A product returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective) is a 3.

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Product works poorly, has major flaws, or is a major disappointment. The product is fundamentally unreliable, a constant hassle, or barely functional.
- **Contradictory Reviews:** If a review describes a major functional failure (e.g., 'freezes the drink solid') but ends with a confusing positive phrase ('but it works'), the failure is the dominant signal. This indicates a deeply flawed product and is typically a 2-star rating.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction. The product is considered non-functional, defective, or useless.
- **Useless or Defective:** The product is broken, unsafe, doesn't work at all, or was returned for a defect. The user may mention "giving up" on it or having "major problems." An UPDATE changing sentiment to negative overrules any earlier praise.

</DynamicRules>
