<BaseGuidelines>
- You will be given a review for a Home & Kitchen product.
- Your task is to classify the review's overall sentiment into a single star rating from 1 to 5.
- Base your rating on the customer's overall satisfaction with the product itself.
- **Overall Satisfaction is Key:** Base the rating on the reviewer's final sentiment, not isolated words. The conclusion is often most telling.
- **Product, Not Process:** Rate the product itself. Ignore complaints about shipping, sellers, or packaging if the product review is positive.
- **Focus on the Reviewed Item:** If a review compares the purchased product to another, rate the purchased product, not the one it replaced.
- **Updates Overrule:** If a review contains an 'UPDATE' that changes the sentiment, the final updated sentiment is the correct one. This is a critical rule.
- Output only the rating number (e.g., "Rating: 4").
</BaseGuidelines>

<DynamicRules>
### Star Level Details

**5 Stars (Excellent / Delighted):**
- **Core Idea:** Product EXCEEDS expectations or works PERFECTLY with no meaningful complaints. The user is enthusiastic ("love it!", "perfect") or factually satisfied.
- **The 'No Complaints' Rule:** A positive review with zero product complaints is a 5. This includes brief, unenthusiastic reviews like "Works fine" or "No problems, happy with them."
- **Minor Issues are Still 5-Star:** Give 5 stars if overall delight is clear despite minor setup hassles, inherent material properties ("it's cotton, so it needs ironing"), or if the user dismisses a flaw or states a simple workaround ("...the cover is a tad too tall... However, I will use the cover when I set the butter out on the table").
- **Context vs. Quality:** Rate the product's quality, not its target audience. A product described as "best suited for someone setting up a kitchen for the first time" is not a flaw if the product itself is solid.
- **High Praise via Comparison:** A review comparing a product favorably to a top-tier brand (e.g., "almost as good as my Lodge pan") is a 5 if the overall tone is highly satisfied.

**4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with Flaws):**
- **Core Idea:** A good product that meets expectations but has a minor, non-dealbreaker flaw.
- **Minor But Persistent Flaw:** The review is mostly happy but mentions a persistent, non-dealbreaker flaw in function, design, or maintenance (e.g., 'wish it was more absorbent,' 'unusually skinny pillows'). Praise for aesthetics doesn't override a practical flaw.
- **Meets, Not Exceeds, Expectations:** A product praised for simply working correctly, especially in comparison to other failed products (e.g., "This one doesn't leak," "It actually holds its shape"), is a 4. It meets the standard, it doesn't exceed it.

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** Balanced pros and cons; the product works but has *significant* limitations. Often uses phrases like "it's okay, but...".
- **Significant Drawback:** A flaw impairs the product's main function (e.g., 'doesn't fit a lot of jars'), overriding other positive comments like 'appears very sturdy'.
- **Incompatibility Return:** A product returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective) is a 3.

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Primarily negative; a significant disappointment. The product is not completely useless, but works poorly, has major flaws, or is a hassle. Unfavorable comparisons are common.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction. The product is considered non-functional, defective, or useless.
- **Useless or Defective:** The product is broken, unsafe, erratic, doesn't work at all, or was returned for a defect. The user may mention "giving up" on it, throwing it away, or having "major problems." This rating applies even if the review started positive but an UPDATE changed the sentiment to negative.

</DynamicRules>
