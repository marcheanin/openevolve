<BaseGuidelines>
- You will be given a review for a Home & Kitchen product.
- Your task is to classify the review's overall sentiment into a single star rating from 1 to 5.
- Base your rating on the customer's overall satisfaction with the product itself.
- **Overall Satisfaction is Key:** Base the rating on the reviewer's final sentiment, not isolated words. The conclusion is often most telling.
- **Product, Not Process:** Rate the product itself. Ignore complaints about shipping, sellers, or packaging if the product review is positive.
- **Focus on the Reviewed Item:** If a review compares the purchased product to another, rate the purchased product, not the one it replaced.
- **Updates Overrule:** If a review contains an 'UPDATE' that changes the sentiment, the final updated sentiment is the correct one.
- Output only the rating number (e.g., "Rating: 4").
</BaseGuidelines>

<DynamicRules>
### Star Level Details

**5 Stars (Excellent / Delighted):**
- **Core Idea:** Product EXCEEDS expectations or works PERFECTLY with no meaningful complaints. The user is enthusiastic ("love it!", "perfect") or factually satisfied.
- **The 'No Complaints' Rule:** A positive review with zero product complaints is a 5. This includes reviews that say "Nothing extraordinary..." but conclude with "no problems" or "I'm happy with it." Do not downgrade for lack of effusive language.
- **Minor Issues are Still 5-Star:** Give 5 stars if overall delight is clear despite minor setup hassles, inherent material properties ("it's cotton, so it needs ironing"), or if the user dismisses a flaw or states a simple workaround ("...but once it's set up, it works great!").
- **Explicit Rating Override:** If a reviewer lists pros and cons but explicitly concludes by giving "5 stars", trust their stated rating.
- **High Praise via Comparison:** A review comparing a product favorably to a top-tier brand (e.g., "almost as good as my Lodge pan") is a 5 if the overall tone is highly satisfied, even if it's not judged "better".
- **Context vs. Quality:** Rate the product's quality, not its target audience. A product described as "perfect for a first apartment" or "great for kids" is not a flaw if the product itself is solid.

**4 Stars (Good / Satisfied with Flaws):**
- **Core Idea:** A good product that meets expectations but has a minor, non-dealbreaker flaw.
- **Minor But Persistent Flaw:** The review is mostly happy but mentions a persistent, non-dealbreaker flaw in function, design, or maintenance (e.g., 'wish it was more absorbent,' 'needs ironing to look good'). Praise for aesthetics doesn't override a practical flaw.
- **Hesitation or Mixed Subjective Feedback:** A positive review that expresses a hypothetical concern ("My only concern would be...") or notes a subjective feature was divisive ("not a universal hit") indicates a reservation preventing a perfect 5-star rating.
- **Meets, Not Exceeds, Expectations:** A product praised for simply working correctly, especially in comparison to other failed products (e.g., "This one doesn't leak," "It actually holds its shape"), is a 4. It meets the standard, it doesn't exceed it.

**3 Stars (Average / Mixed):**
- **Core Idea:** Balanced pros and cons; the product works but has *significant* limitations. Often uses phrases like "it's okay, but...".
- **Significant Drawback:** A flaw impairs the product's main function (e.g., 'shrinks a lot', 'too thin to be useful'), overriding aesthetic praise.
- **Limited Use:** A product described as only "fine for short-term use" or that won't last long is a classic 3.
- **Incompatibility Return:** A product returned because it was the wrong size, didn't fit, or was otherwise incompatible (without being defective) is a 3, not a 1.

**2 Stars (Poor / Disappointed):**
- **Core Idea:** Primarily negative; a significant disappointment.
- **Functional but Flawed:** The product works, but poorly or with major flaws. It is not completely useless, broken, or defective.

**1 Star (Awful / Angry):**
- **Core Idea:** Complete dissatisfaction, product is considered non-functional.
- **Useless or Defective:** The product is broken, unsafe, doesn't work at all, or was returned for a defect. Often includes warnings like "do not buy."

</DynamicRules>
