<DynamicRules>
    ### Star Level Definitions ###

    **5 Stars (Very Positive / Excellent):**
    - The user is delighted; the product meets or exceeds expectations. Keywords: "love," "perfect," "excellent," "highly recommend."
    - A product described as working perfectly or being flawless is a 5, even if the review is short and lacks superlatives.
    - The product solves a long-standing user problem (e.g., "finally found one that doesn't rust").
    - A tiny flaw is mentioned but explicitly dismissed by a highly satisfied user (e.g., "cord is short, but that's a tiny issue for a machine this good").

    **4 Stars (Positive / Good):**
    - The user is satisfied, but with minor reservations or a minor, un-dismissed flaw.
    - Expresses positive but not ecstatic sentiment. Short reviews with general praise ("Awesome pan!") but no detail on *why* it's exceptional are often 4s.
    - Purely descriptive reviews with no complaints are typically 4 stars (e.g., "The color is accurate and the material is sturdy").
    - Reviews that focus primarily on aesthetics ("beautiful design") but describe the function as merely adequate are 4 stars.

    **3 Stars (Neutral / Mixed):**
    - The user has balanced feelings; the product has both significant pros and cons.
    - The product works, but with notable limitations or reliability issues that make the user hesitant (e.g., "I'm hesitant to put anything heavy on it").
    - A review mentioning a potential design flaw that could be a safety concern (e.g., "I can imagine your hand slipping") should be rated 3 stars, even if no accident occurred.

    **2 Stars (Negative / Disappointing):**
    - The user is dissatisfied; negative aspects clearly outweigh the positive.
    - The product has significant functional flaws, is poor quality, or doesn't work as described. Keywords: "disappointed," "cheap," "would not recommend."

    **1 Star (Very Negative / Terrible):**
    - The user is angry or completely dissatisfied. The product is considered junk, non-functional, or dangerous.
    - Any mention of a safety or health hazard (e.g., "fire hazard," "peeling into my food") is a 1-star rating.

</DynamicRules>
