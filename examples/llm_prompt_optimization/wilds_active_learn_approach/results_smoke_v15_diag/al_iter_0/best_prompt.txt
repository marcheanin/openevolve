<DynamicRules>
    ### Star Level Definitions ###

    **5 Stars (Very Positive / Excellent):**
    - The user is delighted, enthusiastic, or highly satisfied. The product meets or exceeds expectations.
    - Look for strong positive language like "love," "perfect," "excellent," "amazing," "highly recommend."
    - A review describing flawless performance is a 5, even without superlatives.
    - If the product perfectly solves a stated problem for the user (e.g., "I could never find one big enough until this one"), it's a strong indicator for 5 stars.

    **4 Stars (Positive / Good):**
    - The user is satisfied overall, but with minor reservations. The product works well but isn't perfect.
    - The review is mostly positive but mentions a minor flaw that doesn't undermine the product's main function (e.g., "a small annoyance, but otherwise solid").
    - **Crucially, short, unenthusiastic but positive reviews like "It works," "Does the job," or "No complaints" are 4 stars.**

    **3 Stars (Neutral / Mixed):**
    - The user has balanced or mixed feelings. The product has both significant pros and cons.
    - It works, but with notable limitations that prevent a positive recommendation (e.g., "looks nice, but doesn't absorb water").
    - **Do not assign 3 stars simply because a positive review is short or lacks enthusiastic language.** A 3-star rating requires substantial negative points to balance the positive ones.

    **2 Stars (Negative / Disappointing):**
    - The user is dissatisfied. The negative aspects clearly outweigh the positive.
    - The product has significant functional flaws, is poor quality, or doesn't work as described. The user expresses disappointment and would not recommend it.
    - Look for phrases like "disappointed," "cheap," "pain to use," "wish I'd bought a different one."

    **1 Star (Very Negative / Terrible):**
    - The user is angry or completely dissatisfied. The product is considered junk, a scam, or non-functional.
    - Any mention of a safety hazard (e.g., "fire hazard," "dangerous") strongly indicates a 1-star rating.
    - Look for clear warnings like "do not buy," "garbage," "returned immediately." A product arriving broken or failing on first use is a 1.

</DynamicRules>
</System>
